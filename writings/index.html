Writing Samples

Writing is an important form of communication. Sometimes I revisit old work and well written reports document the intent at the time, preserving context and knowledge. I value clarity and conciseness with a focus on presentation, so I always use a local copy of the MiKTeX distribution and the TeXstudio editor for full control.

Here is a curated list of technical writings organized by the academic programs they were completed under.

MITx’s Statistical Modeling and Computation in Applications

This course is an online version of MIT’s on-campus capstone for Statistics and Data Science minors and focuses around 4-5 areas of statistical analysis. The problems are assigned, but the approach is unspecified and subject to our individual creativity.

Report 1: Statistics Review

The introductory chapter whets our appetite with a review of the previous course, but with a focus on social experiment ethics, experimental design and interpretation of double blind studies. Studies of Salk’s polio vaccine served as the case study. We also studied the cherry-picking of a large number of hypotheses, a practice rampant in machine learning for example, and how to correct for their p-values with the family-wise error rate (FWER).

Statistics Review (PDF)

Report 2: Genomics

Biological analysis suffers from a curse of high dimensionality due to the sheer amount of genes and other variables in the complex system of life. This not only slows down computation, but also makes it difficult to visualize. This report examines three methods of dimensionality reduction (PCA, MDS, and T-SNE), their effects on visualization, and what happens when we tune some of the hyperparameters. The data set is a subset of tasic2008 on mouse brain cell sequencing, curated by the Allen Institute.

Genomics (PDF)

Report 3: Network Analysis

This report analyzes different phases of a wiretapping investigation into the Serero gang. The goal was to discover the most important members through different measures of centrality (degree, eigenvector, betweenness, hub, and authority), along with some insights about how certain crimes are committed. There is a particular focus on visualization and interpretation, and we were free to choose which crimes we wished to form our hypotheses on. The Project CAVIAR data set is made available by UCINET.

Network Analysis (PDF)

Report 4: Time Series Analysis

This report analyzes Mauna Loa CO2 concentration and, separately, CPI inflation data with the moving average (MA) and autoregression (AR) models, culminating to their combination, the SARIMAX. A final project of sorts gives us the opportunity to experiment and develop our own model, and report on the process of developing the model and the error score.

Time Series Analysis (PDF)

UCLA’s Data Mining

Yelp Ratings Model

The course project was to use a subset of the Yelp Dataset (with some data changed to prevent cheating) to predict Yelp rating on a test set. A private class-wide Kaggle competition was hosted, in which I achieved the top score for the class. Granted, the grad students were likely focused on their thesis, but I was still proud of the accomplishment. The final model used a stacked ensemble of CatBoost and others. More details

Yelp Ratings Class Kaggle (PDF)

UCLA’s Ethical Writing

Norian XR Case Study

Norian XR is a controversial bone cement product used to treat compression fractures that became notorious for causing blood clots. This report examines the ethical and engineering failures of the parent company Synthes, and applies an ethical analysis using utilitarian and deontology frameworks.

Norian XR Ethical Analysis (PDF)

Drones Group Project

The quarter-long course project involved the class to break off into teams and select a topic of their choice, then report the predominant effects of this new technology and potential ethical concerns. I was responsible for the chapter on drone surveillance (as per the included RAM chart) and I was the final editor to ensure cohesion between the sections and that the presentation was well-formatted.

Drones Ethical Analysis (PDF)

UCLA’s Robotics Capstone

UCLA’s capstone for electrical engineering majors was a two-quarter course series that first developed background on a subject, and allowed groups to develop a project and report on progress and results.

Laundry Folding Robots Group Project

Our group tried our hand on using a pair of communicating robots to fold laundry. Course constraints required that we build everything from the ground up, but that total cost be under $100. Needless to say, this presented particular engineering challenges, as documented in the report. Our group members contributed evenly to various tasks, but I wrote most of the report, and compiled and formatted the sections.

Laundry Folding Robots (PDF)

UCLA’s Programming Languages

Atomic Integers

I developed an implementation to atomic integers using Java’s ReentrantLock class and reported on its speed and reliability.

Atomic Integers (PDF)

Tensorflow Bindings

Tensorflow was, at the time, primarily used with Python, although today it has stable bindings for a number of 3rd party languages. This report examines the features of several languages to determine which would be, in my opinion, most suitable for Tensorflow bindings.

Tensorflow Bindings (PDF)
